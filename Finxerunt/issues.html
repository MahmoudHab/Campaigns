Issues that Finxerunt Policy Institute is Currently Solving

Ranging from local communities all the way to international impact the institute is determined to build a sustainable future.

Underserved Communities Healthcare Expansion

Starting in New York City, Finxerunt Policy Institute has pushed over many hospitals in the northeast region and hospitals all across the world. Starting from a $150 Million Dollar this project can reach upto $1 Billion Dollar Internationally.

Reforming the Individualized Education Program

Finxerunt is pushing to reform the current Individualized Education Program. Using award winning policy the proposed reform will help adapt toward the special circumstances from the coronavirus. With politicians, the institution is working to make students' lives better.
STRIPED: Out of Kids’ Hands

Collaboration with Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health and Boston Children’s Hospital to push bills that prohibits minors from consuming harmful weight loss and muscle building supplements in California, Massachusetts, and New York

Reforming the Unemployment Benefit System

Using high-leveled empirical research that analyzes the relation between previous experience with unemployment and location to suitability of the current maximum benefits, Finxerunt is leading the way in redesigning a more accurate and equitable benefit system.
Metropolitan Transportation Authority (MTA) Optimization

The Laboratory published a paper regarding what makes public transportation most efficient in 60 cities. The Laboratory discovered that we can utilize over 300 data points in order to make the best transportation system for the residents of New York City. Together with local politicians, we aim to make sure that the MTA becomes more sustainable and efficient in its operations.
Reestablishing Energy Sourcing in a National Scale

Utilizing over hundreds of responses all across the United States, regarding availability of solar panels, Finxerunt Policy Institute strives to redevelop and reform the foundational source of electricity in the US. Finxerunt is currently planning to reach out to federal politicians to create more accessible solar panels for all US residents, helping build a more environmentally friendly energy base.

Bringing All Forms of Business Back in New York State

The International Socioeconomics Laboratory has conducted a study examining New Yorkers to find where people felt funds for economic recovery should be directed to most. This study shows which industries New Yorkers believe was hardest hit and requires the most help after this pandemic. In cooperation with local budget officials, Finxerunt plans to design the most effective possible localized recovery legislation to bring business back to the State.
Introducing Medical Accessibility to New York City

Through an analyzed empirical study, focused on exposing the lack of accessibility to hospitals in different neighborhoods in NYC, Finxerunt aims to create a more equitable system of medical facilities for the city. After reviewing responses from New Yorkers, different levels of income directly correlated with the proximity of medical care. Finxerunt will lobby different politicians to build hospitals, to insure that income does not dictate healthcare accessibility.
